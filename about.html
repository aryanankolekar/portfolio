Hi! I'm a third-year undergraduate student of Computer Science and Engineering (Artificial Intelligence) at KLE Technological University. My academic interests lie at the intersection of deep learning, computer vision, and applied AI, where I enjoy the theoretical rigor and experimental hands-on approach of these fields.

Throughout my undergraduate degree, I have worked on a variety of projects and research initiatives. Most significantly, I have contributed to the development of a Forged Document Detector, a hybrid AI model based on Convolutional Neural Networks, Vision Transformers, Optical Character Recognition (OCR), and Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) — capable of detecting both visual and textual forgeries on identity documents.

My coursework has covered subjects like Natural Language Processing (NLP), Reinforcement Learning, and Machine Learning, equipping me with a solid foundation in AI theory and practice. I’ve also presented seminars on emerging architectures, including BART (Bidirectional and Auto-Regressive Transformers).

Outside the classroom, I’m passionate about giving back. I volunteer as an English teacher with U&I, helping underprivileged children, and I’ve mentored students in academics and tech learning paths.

When I'm not working on AI models or mentoring, you’ll probably find me following Formula 1. I’m especially fascinated by how AI and data science can enhance sports analytics and performance modeling in motorsport.

Whether it’s through research, service, or personal curiosity, I believe the most meaningful learning happens at the intersection of curiosity, collaboration, and purposeful work.
